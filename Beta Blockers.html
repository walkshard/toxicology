Beta Blocker Toxicity

"One Pill Can Kill" Warning: A single adult pill (especially propranolol or sotalol) can be lethal to a toddler. Propranolol is highly lipophilic, crossing the blood-brain barrier rapidly to cause seizures and abrupt cardiovascular collapse.

Clinical Presentation

  • Vitals: Hypotension, bradycardia.
  • ECG: PR prolongation, AV blocks, widened QRS (propranolol due to sodium channel blockade), QT prolongation (sotalol due to potassium channel blockade).
  • Metabolic: Hypoglycemia (especially in pediatric patients, as beta blockers inhibit glycogenolysis).
  • Neuro: Altered mental status, coma, seizures (classic for propranolol).

Weight-Based Resuscitation & Antidotes

1. Glucagon

First-line specific antidote. Bypasses the beta-receptor to stimulate adenylate cyclase, increasing intracellular cAMP.

  • Pediatric Dose: 50 mcg/kg (0.05 mg/kg) IV bolus over 1-2 minutes.
  • Infusion: If response is positive, start infusion at the "response dose" per hour (e.g., if 2 mg worked, run at 2 mg/hr).
  • Max Single Dose: 5 mg.
  • Note: Frequently causes vomiting. Premedicate with antiemetics. Depletes hepatic glycogen, so effect may wane over time.

2. High-Dose Insulin Euglycemia Therapy (HDI)

Improves myocardial carbohydrate utilization. Highly effective for profound cardiogenic shock failing glucagon/fluids.

  • Regular Insulin Bolus: 1 Unit/kg IV.
  • Regular Insulin Infusion: Start at 1 Unit/kg/hr IV. Titrate up to 10 Units/kg/hr.
  • Dextrose Bolus: 0.5 g/kg IV (Use D25W or D10W for young children) only if blood glucose is < 200 mg/dL.
  • Dextrose Infusion: Start at 0.5 g/kg/hr IV. Maintain BG 100-250 mg/dL.

3. Vasopressors

Direct alpha/beta agonists to overcome competitive blockade.

  • Epinephrine: Start at 0.05 - 0.1 mcg/kg/min IV. Often preferred for beta-blocker toxicity due to its direct beta-agonist properties.
  • Norepinephrine: Start at 0.05 - 0.1 mcg/kg/min IV.

4. Sodium Bicarbonate (For QRS Widening)

Indicated if QRS is > 100-120 ms (seen with propranolol's sodium channel blockade).

  • Dose: 1 - 2 mEq/kg IV push. Repeat as needed to narrow QRS.

5. Lipid Emulsion Therapy (Intralipid 20%)

Consider for refractory shock involving lipophilic beta blockers (Propranolol, Carvedilol, Nebivolol).

  • Bolus: 1.5 mL/kg IV over 1 minute.
  • Infusion: 0.25 mL/kg/min IV.
