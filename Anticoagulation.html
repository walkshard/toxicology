Anticoagulant Reversal (Life-Threatening Bleed)

Warning: Reversal is indicated ONLY for life-threatening or uncontrolled bleeding (e.g., intracranial hemorrhage, massive GI bleed). Reversal agents carry a high risk of subsequent thrombosis.

Reversal by Agent

1. Warfarin (Coumadin)

  • Vitamin K (Phytonadione): 10 mg IV slow push (over 10-20 min). PO takes too long for active bleeding.
  • 4-Factor Prothrombin Complex Concentrate (Kcentra):
    • INR 2 - 3.9: 25 Units/kg (Max 2500 Units)
    • INR 4 - 6: 35 Units/kg (Max 3500 Units)
    • INR > 6: 50 Units/kg (Max 5000 Units)
  • If Kcentra unavailable: FFP (Fresh Frozen Plasma) 10-15 mL/kg.

2. Factor Xa Inhibitors (Apixaban, Rivaroxaban)

  • Andexanet alfa (Andexxa):
    • Low Dose: 400 mg IV bolus at 30 mg/min, followed by 4 mg/min infusion for 120 mins. (Use if last dose > 8 hrs ago or unknown).
    • High Dose: 800 mg IV bolus at 30 mg/min, followed by 8 mg/min infusion for 120 mins. (Use if last dose < 8 hrs ago).
  • Alternative: 4-Factor PCC (Kcentra) 50 Units/kg IV (Off-label but standard of care if Andexxa unavailable/cost-prohibitive).

3. Direct Thrombin Inhibitor (Dabigatran/Pradaxa)

  • Idarucizumab (Praxbind): 5 g IV total. Given as two separate 2.5 g doses no more than 15 minutes apart.
  • Alternative: 4-Factor PCC 50 Units/kg or activated PCC (FEIBA) 50 Units/kg.

4. Heparin / LMWH (Enoxaparin)

  • Protamine Sulfate:
    • Unfractionated Heparin: 1 mg per 100 units of Heparin administered in the last 2-3 hours. Max 50 mg per dose.
    • LMWH (Enoxaparin): 1 mg per 1 mg of Enoxaparin if given within 8 hours. 0.5 mg per 1 mg if given 8-12 hours ago.
