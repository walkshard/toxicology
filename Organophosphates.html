Organophosphates & Cholinergics

Decontamination Warning: Healthcare providers must wear appropriate PPE to avoid secondary toxicity. Remove all patient clothing and wash skin with soap and water aggressively.

Clinical Presentation

Irreversible inhibition of acetylcholinesterase leads to an overabundance of acetylcholine at muscarinic and nicotinic receptors.

  • Muscarinic (DUMBELS): Diarrhea, Urination, Miosis (pinpoint pupils), Bronchorrhea, Emesis, Lacrimation, Salivation.
  • The Killer B's: Bronchorrhea, Bronchospasm, Bradycardia (primary cause of death).
  • Nicotinic (MTWTF): Muscle fasciculations, Tachycardia (can override bradycardia), Weakness, Tremors, Fasciculations (leading to respiratory failure).

Weight-Based Resuscitation & Antidotes

1. Atropine (For Muscarinic Effects)

Competitively blocks acetylcholine at muscarinic receptors. Has no effect on muscle weakness (nicotinic). Goal: Drying of respiratory secretions. Heart rate and pupil size are NOT the endpoints of resuscitation.

  • Starting Dose: Adult: 1 - 3 mg IV. Pediatric: 0.02 - 0.05 mg/kg IV.
  • Titration (Doubling Dose): If no effect in 3-5 minutes, double the dose (e.g., 2 mg -> 4 mg -> 8 mg). Massive doses (hundreds of mg) may be required.
  • Infusion: Once secretions dry, start an infusion at 10-20% of the total dose required to achieve drying, given per hour.

2. Pralidoxime / 2-PAM (For Nicotinic Effects)

Breaks the bond between the organophosphate and acetylcholinesterase, reactivating the enzyme. Must be given before the bond "ages" (becomes permanent).

  • Adult Dose: 30 mg/kg (usually 1 - 2 grams) IV over 15-30 minutes, followed by an infusion of 8 mg/kg/hr.
  • Pediatric Dose: 20 - 50 mg/kg IV over 15-30 minutes, followed by an infusion of 10 - 20 mg/kg/hr.

3. Seizure Management

  • Benzodiazepines: Midazolam, Lorazepam, or Diazepam standard weight-based dosing. Crucial for both treating and preventing CNS damage.
