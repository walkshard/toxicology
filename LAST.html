Local Anesthetic Systemic Toxicity (LAST)

Warning: AVOID vasopressin, calcium channel blockers, beta blockers, and local anesthetics (like lidocaine for arrhythmias). If using epinephrine, use small doses (≤ 1 mcg/kg). Avoid propofol in patients with cardiovascular instability.

Weight-Based Resuscitation & Antidotes

1. Lipid Emulsion Therapy (Intralipid 20%)

The definitive antidote. Acts as a "lipid sink" to pull lipophilic local anesthetics out of the tissues.

  • Patients < 70 kg:
    • Bolus: 1.5 mL/kg IV over 1 minute.
    • Infusion: 0.25 mL/kg/min IV.
  • Patients ≥ 70 kg:
    • Bolus: 100 mL IV over 1 minute.
    • Infusion: 200 - 250 mL IV over 15-20 minutes.
  • Refractory Arrest: May repeat bolus up to 2 times and double infusion rate to 0.5 mL/kg/min. Max total dose: 12 mL/kg.

2. Seizure Management

Seizures increase acidosis, which worsens LAST.

  • Benzodiazepines: First-line. Midazolam 0.05-0.1 mg/kg IV or Lorazepam 0.05 mg/kg IV.
